> How did you proceed after you had an idea for your business?
I did the following. First was the selection: 1) I collected all my business ideas into a list, 2) selected the ideas that can help at least 1 million people, 3) of which I selected the ideas that were so interesting to me that I could work with them every day for at least 10 years without losing the drive, 4) did a market research for each of them, and took away the weakest ideas, 5) from the remaining ones, I have chosen the one I am most likely to be able to implement. After that was the big brainstorming session: 6) for a few weeks I carried a voice recorder everywhere and wrote down all my thoughts on how to realize the business idea, 7) after that I converted the records to text and built a mind map of it, 8) from the mind map I have built a plan of what I will do next. At this point the "Getting Things Done" method was extremely helpful.

Do a thorough market research as early as possible. #4. Build the MVP yourself. Don't wait for a suitable tech co-founder, but start writing the MVP tomorrow or learn everything you need to know. Nowadays you don't need a university to learn programming.  If you find a good tech co-founder, it is of course wonderful. But such people are even rarer than good spouses.

> Please briefly describe the specific problem you have in mind.
Given the inputs and outputs of the patient's brain, is it possible to create a LLM-based system that will act and think sufficiently similar to the patient?

Immortality by the means of mind uploading (under certain assumptions)

> Why do you believe AlphaEvolve is particularly well-suited to overcome these limitations?
